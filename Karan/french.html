French Cuisine

Appetizers

  1. Escargots

    2. Ingredients:
    • 24 canned snail shells
    • 1 lb escargot meat
    • 4 Tbsp butter
    • 4 Tbsp finely chopped parsley
    • 2 garlic cloves, minced
    • 1/4 cup dry white wine
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    Steps:
    • Rinse the shells and let dry.
    • In a skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add garlic and cook 1 minute.
    • Add escargot, wine, salt and pepper. Cook for 2-3 minutes until heated through.
    • Fill each shell with escargot mixture and top with chopped parsley.
    • Serve escargots while hot with crusty bread.
  2. French Onion Soup

    3. Ingredients:
    • 3 Tbsp butter
    • 3 large onions, sliced
    • 3 cups beef broth
    • 1 cup red wine
    • 1 bay leaf
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • 4 slices French bread
    • 1 cup shredded gruy√®re cheese
    Steps:
    • In a large pot, melt butter over medium heat.
    • Add onions and cook until very soft and caramelized, about 20 minutes.
    • Add broth, wine, bay leaf, salt and pepper. Bring to a boil.
    • Reduce heat and simmer soup for 15 minutes.
    • Ladle soup into oven-safe bowls. Top with a bread slice and cheese.
    • Broil 5 minutes until cheese is melted and browned.
    • Remove bay leaf before serving.

Main Courses

  1. Coq Au Vin

    2. Ingredients:
    • 3 lbs chicken thighs and drumsticks
    • 1 bottle (750 ml) red wine like Burgundy
    • 2 cups chicken broth
    • 8 oz bacon or salt pork, diced
    • 1 onion, diced
    • 2 garlic cloves, minced
    • 1 Tbsp tomato paste
    • 2 bay leaves
    • 1 tsp dried thyme
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    Steps:
    • Pat the chicken dry and season with salt and pepper.
    • In a dutch oven, cook the bacon over medium heat until crisp.
    • Remove bacon bits and add chicken pieces. Brown the chicken on both sides.
    • Remove chicken and set aside. Add onions to bacon drippings and cook 2 minutes.
    • Stir in garlic and tomato paste. Cook 1 minute more.
    • Pour in the wine and broth. Add bay leaves, thyme and bacon bits.
    • Return chicken to pot along with any accumulated juices. Bring to a boil.
    • Reduce heat, cover and simmer for 45 mins until chicken is very tender.
    • With a slotted spoon, transfer chicken to a platter. Simmer sauce until reduced.
    • Remove bay leaves. Season sauce with salt and pepper to taste.
    • Pour sauce over chicken and serve.
  2. Ratatouille

    3. Ingredients:
    • 1 onion, sliced
    • 1 eggplant, cubed
    • 1 zucchini, sliced
    • 1 yellow squash, sliced
    • 2 bell peppers, chopped
    • 3 garlic cloves, minced
    • 3 Tbsp olive oil
    • 1 (14.5 oz) can diced tomatoes
    • 1/4 cup tomato paste
    • 1 Tbsp herbes de Provence
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    Steps:
    • Heat oil in a skillet over medium heat.
    • Add onions and cook 5 minutes until translucent.
    • Add garlic and cook 1 minute until fragrant.
    • Add eggplant, zucchini, squash and peppers. Cook 5 minutes.
    • Mix in tomatoes, tomato paste and herbs. Season with salt and pepper.
    • Bring to a simmer, cover and cook for 20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
    • Uncover and continue cooking 10 more minutes until vegetables are very tender.
    • Let ratatouille cool slightly before serving.
