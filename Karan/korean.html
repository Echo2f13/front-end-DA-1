Appetizers

  1. Kimchi Pancake

    2. Ingredients:
    • 1 cup kimchi, drained of excess liquid and chopped into bite-sized pieces
    • 1 cup all-purpose flour
    • 1/4 cup water
    • 1 egg, beaten with a fork
    • 1 green onion, trimmed and thinly sliced on the diagonal
    • Vegetable oil for frying
    Steps:
    • In a medium mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, water, beaten egg, chopped kimchi and sliced green onions vigorously until a smooth, lump-free batter forms.
    • Heat 1-2 Tbsp vegetable oil in a nonstick skillet or pan over medium heat. Swirl to coat the bottom.
    • Scoop about 3 Tbsp of batter per pancake into the hot oil. Gently flatten and shape each pancake into a circle with the back of a spoon.
    • Cook for 2-3 minutes per side, until golden brown. Flip carefully with a spatula.
    • Transfer cooked pancakes to a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil. Repeat with remaining batter.
    • Serve the hot kimchi pancakes immediately, garnished with extra sliced green onions if desired.
  2. Mandu (Korean Dumplings)

    3. Ingredients:
    • 1/2 lb ground pork
    • 1/2 lb ground beef
    • 3 green onions, trimmed and finely minced
    • 1 tsp soy sauce
    • 1 tsp toasted sesame oil
    • 30 small round wonton wrappers
    Steps:
    • In a large bowl, thoroughly mix together the ground pork, ground beef, minced green onions, soy sauce and sesame oil using your hands until well combined.
    • Place a wonton wrapper diagonally on a clean, dry work surface. Scoop approximately 1 tbsp of filling into the center.
    • Use your finger to brush the edges of the wrapper lightly with water. Fold the wrapper in half to form a triangle, pressing firmly along the edges to seal tightly.
    • Place the filled mandu on a baking sheet lightly dusted with flour, seam side up. This prevents sticking.
    • Steam the mandu in a single layer in a bamboo steamer lined with parchment for 12-15 minutes until filling is cooked through.

Main Courses

  1. Bulgogi (Korean BBQ Beef)

    2. Ingredients:
    • 1 lb flank steak, sliced across the grain into 1/8" thick slices
    • 3 garlic cloves, minced to a paste
    • 1/4 cup soy sauce
    • 2 Tbsp brown sugar
    • 2 tsp toasted sesame oil
    • 1 onion, halved and sliced thin
    Steps:
    • In a shallow dish, combine garlic, soy sauce, brown sugar and sesame oil. Add beef slices in a single layer and turn to coat completely. Marinate for 30 minutes at room temperature.
    • Heat a cast iron skillet or grill pan over medium-high heat. Working in batches if needed, cook beef and onions for 2-3 minutes per side, flipping once, until lightly charred.
    • Transfer cooked bulgogi to a serving platter. Serve immediately with steamed rice and ssamjang sauce on the side.
  2. Bibimbap

    3. Ingredients:
    • 4 cups steamed white rice
    • 1 cup spinach, trimmed and blanched
    • 1 carrot, julienned into matchsticks
    • 1 zucchini, julienned into matchsticks
    • 8 oz ground beef, cooked
    • 2 Tbsp gochujang (Korean chili paste)
    • 1 egg
    • Toasted sesame oil
    Steps:
    • Heat a skillet with 1 tsp oil over medium-high heat. Saut√© the julienned carrots and zucchini for 2-3 minutes until slightly softened but still crisp.
    • In a separate skillet, cook the ground beef over medium-high heat, breaking it up finely with a spoon as it cooks until no longer pink.
    • Fry an egg sunny-side up in a bit of vegetable oil over medium heat until the white is set but the yolk is still runny.
    • Place steamed white rice in the bottom of a bowl. Arrange the sauteed vegetables, cooked ground beef and fried egg attractively over the rice.
    • Drizzle 1 Tbsp gochujang sauce and 1 tsp sesame oil over the top. Mix everything together before eating.
